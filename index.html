Algebraic and Computational Limits of LLM Guardrails

Preprint · Targeting USENIX Security 2027

I got curious about why "read every other character" jailbreaks work against LLM safety filters. Turns out there's an algebraic reason — regex guardrails have aperiodic syntactic monoids, placing them in AC⁰, which provably can't compute modular counting. The paper also covers information-theoretic (Fano bound) and computational (NP-completeness) barriers for inference-layer guardrails. First paper — learned a lot.
