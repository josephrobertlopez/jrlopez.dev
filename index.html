Algebraic and Computational Limits of LLM Guardrails

Under Review — USENIX Security 2027

We prove that LLM guardrails face four structurally distinct barriers: algebraic blindness (syntactic monoid aperiodicity places regex filters in AC⁰, provably unable to compute MOD_p), information-theoretic loss (Fano bound), computational intractability (NP-completeness of instantiation verification), and syntactic indistinguishability of adversarial from legitimate formal reasoning. Validated on 91 regex patterns (97.1% aperiodic) and an N=20 Tree-of-Thought benchmark.

About

I work on applying algebraic automata theory and information theory to AI safety problems. My current focus is on proving structural limitations of LLM guardrail systems — not finding bugs in specific implementations, but establishing mathematical impossibility results for entire classes of defenses.

  • Focus: Syntactic monoids, circuit complexity (AC⁰/TC⁰), Fano bounds, free category functoriality
  • Tools: Python, LaTeX, Claude Code, Ollama, behave (BDD)
  • Methodology: Agentic research pipeline with adversarial AI peer review — the same LLM reasoning capability that enables guardrail bypass also enables research quality gates
